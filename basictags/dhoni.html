Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni (born 7 July 1981) is an Indian former international cricketer and captain of the Indian national team. He is widely regarded as one of the greatest captains in the history of cricket.

Early Life

Dhoni was born in Ranchi, Bihar (now in Jharkhand) to Pan Singh and Devaki Devi. He attended DAV Jawahar Vidya Mandir, Shyamali, where he initially excelled in badminton and football. He was a goalkeeper for his football team and was selected to play for his districtâ€™s cricket club.

Domestic Career

Before making his ODI debut in 2004, Dhoni was part of the Bihar and Jharkhand cricket teams. His impressive performances in the domestic circuit earned him a place in the India A squad.

International Career

One-Day Internationals (ODI)

Dhoni made his ODI debut on 23 December 2004 against Bangladesh. He quickly rose to prominence with his aggressive batting style, including a memorable knock of 183* against Sri Lanka in 2005.

Test Career

He made his Test debut on 2 December 2005 against Sri Lanka and became known for his calm demeanor and leadership skills.

T20 International

Dhoni captained India in their first T20 match in December 2006. Under his leadership, India won the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup in 2007.

Captaincy

Dhoni was appointed as the captain of the Indian team in 2007, leading them to numerous victories including the ICC World T20 (2007), ICC Cricket World Cup (2011), and ICC Champions Trophy (2013).

Indian Premier League (IPL)

Dhoni has been the captain of the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) since the inception of the IPL in 2008, leading them to several championships.

Records and Achievements

  • First captain to win all three ICC limited-overs trophies (World Cup, Champions Trophy, and T20 World Cup).
  • Holds the record for most wins by an Indian captain in ODIs and T20Is.
  • Most successful captain in IPL history with four titles for Chennai Super Kings.

Personal Life

Dhoni married Sakshi Singh Rawat on 4 July 2010. The couple has a daughter named Ziva.

Retirement

On 15 August 2020, Dhoni announced his retirement from international cricket via an Instagram post, ending a remarkable career spanning over 16 years.

Legacy

Dhoni is known for his calm demeanor on the field and his ability to remain composed under pressure, earning him the nickname "Captain Cool". He continues to play in the IPL and is involved in various business ventures.
